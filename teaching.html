Teaching

Good math teaching gives the learner small, structured pieces of expertise and encourages them to creating something new and amazing with it. While guiding students through this process, my bias is toward building metacognitive and communivation skills. In the classroom, this looks like an emphasis on community and peer discussion, with inquiry-based activities, presentations, and think-pair-share activities throughout the hour.

Current and past students can find course materials on the univerity LMS platform or printed out in class.

Teaching at York University

  • Math 2022 (Advanced Linear Algebra) – Spring 2025. An axiomatic treatment of linear algebra for students who have completed a course in matrix-vector methods. Covers abstract vector spaces, linear transformations, coordinates and matrix theory, inner product spaces, and orthogonality.
  • Math 1506 (Calculus for Life Sciences) – Spring 2024. An introduction to differential calculus for science-oriented students, with an emphasis on applications to biology and medicine. Covers limits, continuity, techniques and applications of differentiation.
  • Math 1200 (Problems, Conjectures, and Proofs) – Spring 2023. Introduces first and second year majors to rigorous mathematical proof and communication using topics from logic, algebra, number theory, and combinatorics.
