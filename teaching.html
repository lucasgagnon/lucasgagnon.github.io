Teaching

I teach by giving students small pieces of expertise and challenging them to build something bigger. I find that building strong social ties and community in the classroom is the best catalyst for deep and meaningful learing. I'm also fascinated with metacognition and critical thinking, and encourage my students to question how and what they are learning at all times.

Current and past students can find course materials on the univerity LMS platform (e.g. Brightspace).

Teaching at University of Southern California

  • Math 432 (Applied Combinatorics) – Spring 2026. A first undergraduate course in combinatorics, following A Walk Through Combinatorics (Bona)
  • Math 117 (Precalculus for Business and Economics) – Fall 2025 (x2 sections). A tour of algebraic and exponential functions, covering abstract properties, graphing, and interpreting models.

Teaching at York University

  • Math 2022 (Advanced Linear Algebra) – Spring 2025. An axiomatic treatment of linear algebra for students who have completed a course in matrix-vector methods. Covers abstract vector spaces, linear transformations, coordinates and matrix theory, inner product spaces, and orthogonality.
  • Math 1506 (Calculus for Life Sciences) – Spring 2024. An introduction to differential calculus with an emphasis on applications to biology and medicine. Covers limits, continuity, techniques and applications of differentiation.
  • Math 1200 (Problems, Conjectures, and Proofs) – Spring 2023. Introduces new majors to rigorous mathematical proof and communication using topics from logic, algebra, number theory, and combinatorics.
